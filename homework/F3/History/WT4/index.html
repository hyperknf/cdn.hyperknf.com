The adoption of the appeasement policy by Britain in 1938, aimed at avoiding another devastating war, was ultimately proved to be a bad decision that failed to address the growing threat posed by Nazi germany. The policy ultimately emboldened Hitler, allowed Germany to expand its military capabilities. In this essay, we will discuss the failures of the appeasement policy, highlighting the situation in Europe during the 1930s.

Firstly, the appeasement policy failed to recognize the intentions of Hitler and Nazi Germany. During the 1930s, Germany showed aggression to expand its territorial influence, violating the Treaty of Versailles and annexing Austria and Sudetenland in Czechoslovakia (later the entirety). By accepting Hitler's demands, Britain effectively condoned his actions and underestimated Germany's ambitions, This failure allowed Hitler to solidify his power and strengthen Germany's military capabilities.

Secondly, the appeasement policy not only undermined collective security efforts, but also disregarded the vulnerable nations in Europe. By appeasing Hitler's actions and allowing the annexations of Austria and Czechoslovakia, Britain and other powers neglected the sovereignty and security of these nations. The policy failed to uphold international law and territorial integrity, and telling the world that small nations could be sacrificed for the sake of appeasement. this disregard for the rights of small nations not only weakened international relations, but also emboldened Hitler further, as he saw the lack of resistance to his actions and ambitions.

Supporters of the policy may argue that it provided Britain with the needed time to rearm and prepare for war. Britain's military was significantly weaker than Germany's at the time and appeasement might have offered a temporary respite for enhancing its defense. A confrontational approach might also have resulted in an earlier war.

While that might be true, the appeasement policy was a flawed strategy in achieving this objective. By allowing Hitler to continue his aggressive actions, the policy enabled Germany to further strengthen its military desire. The delay brought by the policy provided Hitler with valuable time to reinforce his power, expand his army, and build alliances, thereby increasing the military imbalance between Britain and Germany.

In conclusion. the adoption of the appeasement policy by Britain in 1938 was an unwise decision that failed to prevent war and protect Europe's security. By underestimating the true intentions of Hitler and Nazi Germany, Britain indirectly contributed to the escalation of World War II, which broke out later in 1939. The policy ultimately demonstrated a lack of foresight and failure to recognize the situation, resulting in a disastrous war.
